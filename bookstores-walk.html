A walk along Central London's independent bookstores

This is a route I have plotted along some of London's independent bookstores. The walk itself is a little less than an hour, and takes you all the way from Regent's canal in King's Cross, through Bloomsbury, the part of town long known for its bookshop and literary tradition, and down to Charing Cross in the heart of London's West End.

Open route in Google Maps

1. Word On The Water - The London Bookbarge

This floating bookstore is moored on Regent's Canal towpath at King's Cross and stacked with rows of "new and used classics, cult, contemporary fiction and a large range of children's books."

2. Housmans Bookshop

The first of two radical bookstores on this list, this not-for-profit bookstore has been running since 1945, selling new and secondhand publications on progressive politics.

3. Judd Books

Trading for over 25 years, Judd Books has over 50,000 new and used books in the arts and social sciences.

4. Skoob Books

Skoob is one of London's largest second-hand bookstores, with thousands of uncatalogued academic works.

5. London Review Bookshop

This popular bookstore is well-stocked with new releases on a wide range of subjects.

6. Jarndyce Booksellers

Jarndyce is a specialist bookstore in 18th and 19th century English literature and history.

7. Bookmarks Bookshop

My personal favorite, Bookmarks is a socialist bookstore with a range of books on everything from politics to economics, the environment, and radical children's books.

8. Any Amount of Books

This exceptional, old-fashioned bookstore stocks a large collection of used and rare books.
